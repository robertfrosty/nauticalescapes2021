Our Boats

Come see our collection of floating homes, grocery getters, and fishing vessels that we've owned and operated over the years. If you are looking for any other information, feel free to check out our contact us page.

Our Song 2004 Mainship 400 Trawler

We chose a Trawler so we could take the leisurely route, often cruising at 7-10 knots. She's economical and very comfortable.
Trawlers are a very distinct type of power boat, designed for long distance cruising and in many cases, living aboard for significant stretches of time.

Beautiful, spacious flybridge; perfect for entertaining guests and barbequing.

Cummins 380 Horsepower Diesel and 9kW Onan generator, combined with the watermaker and Garmin navigational equipment provides economical cruising and live-aboard.

Gorgeous cherry-wood interior all throughout, and two staterooms make living aboard a dream.

My Condo 1983 Uniflite 46 Motor Yacht

This was our first experience with a boat of this size; The entire boat was very spacious, including the flybridge which provided lots of room for guests

This Flybridge had a second helm, barbeque and lots of seating. It's a great perch to navigate from.

Twin Detroit Diesel 671's propelled us around the Great Lakes with a top speed of 18 knots.

Beautiful teak hardwood all throughout the triple stateroom layout,that included two heads.

Nite Dreams 1976 Pacemaker 32 Sedan Cruiser

This vessel was a first for many things, such as a Flybridge, twin drives, sleeping quarters etc. Comfortable boat for small gatherings.

Very comfortable vessel. Size was perfect for day trips and weekend get-aways.

Twin Crusader 305's propelled the Pacemaker along at 15 Knots, and played a large role in developing our love for boats.

Beautiful teak throughout. The galley was set up to make great use of small space. Slept 4 adults comfortably.

A Day In The Life

Our Lifestyle and Beliefs

Healthy Living

Eating healthy, staying active, and maintaing positive mental health habits are all cornerstones in the foundation of our life. We catch & eat fish, staying with our pesquatarian lifestyle. Swimming, yoga and walks are a daily thing when we're on shore.

Sustainable Living

This planet is the only one we have, and it's our responsibility to take care of it, and preserve it for future generations. We aim to recycle, reuse and always pick up garbage whether it's in the water or on shore. You would not believe how many balloons we pick up on the water.

Onboard Living

Living a one hundred percent onboard lifestyle can be a daunting, but rewarding task to take on. Our onboard watermaker gives us fresh water no matter where we are, and we have a herb garden with tomatoe plants for fresh greens and veggies.
